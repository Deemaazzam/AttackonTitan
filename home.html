  • Author's Biography

    Hajime Isayama is a Japanese manga artist, best known as the creator of the manga series "Attack on Titan" (Shingeki no Kyojin). He was born on August 29, 1986, in Hita, Oita Prefecture, Japan. Isayama began drawing manga in his early teens, and after graduating from high school, he enrolled in a manga design program at a vocational school in Fukuoka. He initially struggled to find success as a manga artist, but he eventually gained recognition for his short manga series "Heart Break One," which was published in the Bessatsu Shonen Magazine in 2006. In 2009, Isayama began working on "Attack on Titan," a post-apocalyptic manga series set in a world where humanity lives in cities surrounded by massive walls to protect themselves from giant humanoid creatures known as Titans. The series has become incredibly popular both in Japan and internationally, with millions of copies sold worldwide and numerous adaptations in various media, including anime, video games, and live-action films. Isayama has received several awards and nominations for his work on "Attack on Titan," including the Kodansha Manga Award for best shonen manga in 2011 and the Harvey Award for best manga in 2014. He is known for his dark and mature storytelling, as well as his detailed and dynamic artwork.
